Hello, my name is Nichos Molnar.

and this is a little about me and a lot about my new year's resolution.

I grew up in Oakland, California, and have studied and worked in biology research for the past few years. I have a strong love for nature, learning about useful or useless things, and for storytelling. I grew up reading National Geographic and I have a strong fondness for comics and other combinations of text and image.

Right now, I'm working on my new year's resolution: draw the 14 tallest mountains in the world.

Around Thanksgiving, I finished Into Thin Air, an account of a mountaneering disaster on the slopes of Mount Everest. This book set my brain on fire. I sent myself down a rabbit hole, researching Everest, mountaineering, mountaineering disasters, and 14 "eight-thousanders," an elite club made up of the tallest, most remote mountains in the world. Like so many before me, I am fascinated by these peaks. For the moment, my interest is limited such that I can enjoy the mountains firmly from the safety of my home and away from the dangers of those altitudes.

Coincidentally, I was learning how to draw, and was looking for subjects to paint. Mountains are good subject material for learning shapes, light, and color. So I set my goal: 14 mountains, 12 months. Something achievable!
